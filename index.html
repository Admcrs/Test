The Longest River:The Nile River, the world's longest river, flows through Egypt. The Pyramids: The Great Pyramids of Giza are the only remaining Ancient Wonders of the World. The Sphinx: The Sphinx, a massive limestone statue with a lion's body and a human head, guards the pyramids. Mummification: The ancient Egyptians believed in an afterlife and mummified their pharaohs and nobles to preserve their bodies for the journey. Hieroglyphics: The ancient Egyptians developed a complex writing system called hieroglyphics. Cats: Cats were considered sacred animals in ancient Egypt. The Statue of Liberty: The Statue of Liberty was originally intended for the Suez Canal in Egypt but was later gifted to the United States. The Red Sea: Egypt shares a coastline with the Red Sea, a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling. The Sahara Desert: Egypt is home to a large portion of the Sahara Desert, the world's largest hot desert. Ancient Medicine: The ancient Egyptians were pioneers in medicine, developing treatments for various diseases and performing complex surgeries.
